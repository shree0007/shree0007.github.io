Certifications

Introduction to the EU Taxonomy and Sustainable Finance

Through this certification, I gained a clear understanding of how the EU Taxonomy supports sustainable finance and how it connects with the Paris Agreement, the EU Green Deal, CSRD & ESRS, and SFDR in driving the transition to a sustainable economy. I learned about the Delegated Acts, NACE codes, the six environmental objectives, how to identify taxonomy-eligible and aligned activities, and key concepts like TSC, DNSH, MSS, as well as KPIs (such as Turnover, CapEx, OpEx, GAR, FinGuAR, AUM) for both companies and financial institutions. This certification also gave me insights into the best practices and challenges of applying the EU Taxonomy in practice.

Deep dive into the CSRD & ESRS for corporates

During this certification program provided by Greenomy Academy, I gained a thorough understanding of key sustainability-related regulations, frameworks, and reporting standards. The program primarily focused on the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) and the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), while also covering the EU Green Deal, Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD), EU Taxonomy, Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR), and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) principles. In addition to regulatory knowledge, the certification deepened my understanding of essential sustainability concepts such as double materiality, materiality assessment, gap analysis, due diligence, Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) , Principal Adverse Impacts (PAIs) etc. This training provided a solid foundation for navigating the evolving landscape of sustainable finance and corporate sustainability reporting.
